## Introduction

The FAQ section allows you to publish answers to common customer questions that appear on your checkout page. When customers click the FAQ button during payment, they can quickly find important information without leaving the checkout flow.

Access FAQ from **Brand Settings → FAQ** to manage your frequently asked questions.

**Where FAQs appear:**  
Checkout page FAQ button (visible to all customers during payment)

**Who sees them:**  
Customers making payments through your checkout, payment links, or invoices

## Create FAQ

Click **New FAQ** to add a frequently asked question.

**Title** (required)  
The customer's question.

- Write as a question customers would ask
- Keep clear and specific
- Examples: "How long does payment verification take?", "What payment methods do you accept?", "Can I get a refund?"

**Content** (required)  
Your answer to the question.

- Provide clear, concise information
- Answer the question completely
- Include timeframes or specifics when relevant
- Keep language simple and customer-friendly

**Status** (toggle)  
Enable to show immediately on checkout.

- Toggle on (blue) — FAQ visible to customers
- Toggle off (gray) — FAQ saved but hidden
- Can be changed after creation

### Example FAQ

**Title:**  
How long does verification take?

**Content:**  
Most payments are verified instantly. Some banks may take up to 5 minutes. If your payment isn't verified after 10 minutes, please contact our support team.

## Edit FAQ

Click **Edit** from the actions menu to modify an existing FAQ.

**Editable fields:**
- **Title** — Update the question
- **Content** — Revise the answer
- **Status** — Enable/disable visibility

Changes take effect immediately on the checkout page after saving.

## Reorder FAQs

Control the order FAQs appear to customers on the checkout page.

**To reorder:**
1. Click the reorder icon (drag handle) on an FAQ
2. Drag the FAQ up or down in the list
3. Drop in the desired position
4. Order saves automatically

The order in your list is the exact order customers see on checkout.

::: tip
Place the most common and important questions at the top:
1. Payment methods accepted
2. Verification time
3. Refund policy
4. Security information
5. Contact support

Less common questions should appear lower in the list.
:::

## Best Practices

**Writing questions:**
- Use customer language (how they would ask)
- Be specific and clear
- Avoid technical jargon
- One topic per question

**Writing answers:**
- Keep responses concise (2-4 sentences)
- Answer the question directly
- Include specific timeframes or numbers
- Avoid vague terms like "soon" or "quickly"

**Content guidelines:**
- Include only payment-related information
- Don't publish sensitive details (account numbers, passwords)
- Link to your website for detailed policies
- Keep tone professional but friendly

**Organization:**
- Most asked questions at the top
- 5-8 FAQs is ideal (too many overwhelms)
- Group related questions together
- Remove outdated or duplicate questions

**Maintenance:**
- Review quarterly for accuracy
- Update timeframes and policies
- Remove questions customers no longer ask
- Add new questions based on support inquiries

**Common FAQ topics:**
- Accepted payment methods
- Verification timeframes
- Refund policies
- Security measures
- Transaction fees
- Payment proof requirements
- Customer support contact

## Troubleshooting

**FAQ not visible on checkout**

Possible causes:
- Status is toggled off (disabled)
- FAQ was deleted
- Browser cache showing old version

**Solution:**
- Check Active toggle is on (blue)
- Clear browser cache and refresh
- Verify FAQ still exists in list

**Wrong display order**

FAQs appear in different order than expected.

**Solution:**
- Use drag-and-drop to reorder
- Refresh checkout page to verify
- Check if multiple admins are editing simultaneously

**Typo or incorrect information**

Answer contains outdated or wrong information.

**Solution:**
- Click Edit on the FAQ
- Update content
- Save changes
- Changes appear immediately

**Too many FAQs**

Checkout FAQ section looks cluttered.

**Solution:**
- Disable less common questions
- Keep only 5-8 most important
- Combine related questions
- Delete duplicate or outdated FAQs

::: tip
A few clear, well-written FAQs are more helpful than many long, complicated ones. Focus on quality over quantity to help customers complete payments faster.
:::

::: warning
Changes to FAQ content appear immediately on the checkout page. Always review your changes before saving to avoid publishing errors or incorrect information to customers.
:::
